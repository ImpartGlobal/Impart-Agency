## 0. TL;DR

The current site positions Impart as a **generalist results-driven digital
agency**. The brief is to reposition it as **the AI agency for South Africa**,
with a path to global. That is a fundamental pivot, not a copy polish. Every
surface below needs to either say "AI" or earn its place as supporting
evidence (case studies, process, team, trust).

Three things have to change first:

1. **Hero** — headline, sub, social proof strip, primary CTA.
2. **Services architecture** — AI moves from "one of nine" to the spine;
   everything else becomes "supporting capability".
3. **Proof** — AI-specific case studies, team, and stack visibility. Without
   these, the repositioning is a claim without evidence, which is exactly
   what Hormozi would flag as the #1 reason an agency site fails.

Pricing recommendation at the end: **hide fixed prices, move to
contact-for-quote** for AI work. Top AI and consulting firms (BCG X,
Kearney, Slalom, Deloitte AI Institute, Thoughtworks, Fractal) all do this.
Pricing tiers are a generalist-agency signal.

information architecture below is organised around it.

## 7. Team profiles — yes, and here's how

**Question:** does a top-tier AI agency show team profiles?

**Answer:** yes, at the senior level. Top AI consultancies (Slalom, BCG X,
Fractal, Thoughtworks) all feature named leaders. Boutique AI agencies
(Zion AI, Gradient, Human Loop) go further and feature the whole team. The
signal matters more in AI than in general agency work, because buyers want
to know they're paying for real engineers and not a white-labelled
subcontractor.

**Recommendation:**

- **New `/team` page.** 3–6 profiles at launch (you + principals). Each
  profile: headshot, name, role, 1-sentence positioning, LinkedIn link, and
  2–3 AI projects they've led.
- **Home page** gets a `Team` section showing the same 3–6 cards, below
  "Why Impart".
- **About page** links to Team.

## 8. Pricing — my recommendation: hide it

The site currently has `app/pricing/page.tsx` with 9 tiered pricing cards
(project-based ranges in ZAR). For a generalist agency, this is great — it
pre-qualifies buyers and reduces sales load. For an AI agency positioned at
the top of the market, **it's a liability**:

- It anchors you to SMB pricing when AI engagements are enterprise-scale.
- None of the agencies you want to be compared to (BCG X, Slalom,
  Thoughtworks, Fractal, boutique AI shops like Gradient / Zion) publish
  prices.
- The few that do (Elogic, some AI SaaS-adjacent agencies) are positioned
  as productised, not bespoke.
- You'll lose the deals where the buyer has a R1m+ budget, because the
  site has told them this is a R30k–R80k shop.

**Proposed structure:**

- **Remove `/pricing` from primary nav.**
- Replace with a **"How We Engage"** page that describes engagement *shapes*
  (AI Audit, Scoped POC, Implementation Programme, Ongoing AI Ops) without
  prices. Each shape has a duration range and an outcome — no rand figures.
- Every engagement shape ends in a "Request a proposal" CTA.
- Keep the existing `content/pricing.ts` in the repo (don't delete) but
  stop rendering it on the public site until we decide otherwise. This is
  reversible if data shows we need productised tiers back.

## 10. Graphics and imagery — what you need to source

For an agency site at the level you want, the current image-free aesthetic
will read as under-invested. Top AI agency sites lean heavily on one of
three visual directions:

1. **Abstract generative / editorial illustration** (Gradient.io, Humans In
   The Loop)
2. **Dark techy 3D with brand accents** (Thoughtworks, Runway)
3. **Editorial photography of the team + work** (Slalom, BCG X)

Recommendation: **direction 2 + light direction 3**. It ladders into your
current dark-theme + brand-orange design without a redesign.

### What to pull from Envato / Adobe Stock / Unsplash

**Hero / above-the-fold:**
- 1× dark abstract "neural / data flow" visual (video loop or static) —
  must work at 1920×1080 and cropped to mobile.
- 2× "product-surface" mockup frames (laptop + dashboard, phone + chat UI)
  showing example AI output. Generic enough to be non-specific-client.

**Service pages (5 services × 1 hero image each):**

**H1:** Our 90-Day Guarantee

**Sub:** AI systems that earn their keep in the first 90 days — or we
keep working for free until they do.

**Body, section by section:**

#### What we promise

Every Impart engagement ships with a measurable business outcome tied to
the engagement shape. If the system we build hasn't earned its keep by
day 90 — defined as the measurable outcome you and we agreed on before we
started — we keep working, at our cost, until it does.

#### What "earning its keep" means

We agree the metric at kickoff. It's specific, tied to revenue or cost,
and measurable on your existing dashboards. We don't make up the metric
after the fact. Examples of metrics we've used:

- "The agent deflects X% of inbound support tickets."
- "The AI-generated content drives Y% more qualified traffic."
- "The internal copilot saves Z hours per analyst per week."

#### What's covered

- Systems we design, build, and deploy as part of the engagement.
- The specific, agreed outcome metric.
- A reasonable correction window — typically 30 days beyond the original
  engagement end.

#### What's not covered

- Outcome shifts caused by changes on your side (new org structure, new
  product launch, policy changes that weren't in scope at kickoff).
- Infrastructure or licence costs. You pay what the cloud and model
  vendors charge; we don't mark those up, and we don't refund them.
- Engagements where the outcome metric was never agreed in writing. We
  won't start work without one.

#### The small print (in English)

- Every engagement has this guarantee in the statement of work. It's not
  a marketing line — it's contractual.
- "Free" means zero fees to Impart. You still pay infrastructure and
  licence costs for AI systems that are still running.
- If after a correction window it's clear the metric can't be hit —
  because the hypothesis was wrong, not the execution — we'll agree to
  wind down and refund the unused portion of the engagement.
- We reserve the right to decline an engagement where the outcome metric
  is unmeasurable, unrealistic, or outside our technical control. We'd
  rather walk away on day one than set you up to lose.

#### Why we do this

The AI space is full of decks and pilots that never ship. We'd rather
earn the work twice than collect once on a project that doesn't land.
That's the whole business model.
